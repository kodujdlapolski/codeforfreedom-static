A Vote Counts a Lot. Join Us in Building a Bosnian Open Elections Data Portal!

Anna Kuliberda, Darko Brkan, Feđa Kulenović, Maciej Biłas and Alicja Peszkowska - a team which worked on building the portal up in Sarajevo a month ago is coming to Gdańsk.

 

This small yet strong gang of representatives including project leaders from Zasto ne – a Bosnian watchdog organization, is just a part of a wider group that worked on the project. Open Knowledge Foundation School of Data, engine room, Tactical Technology Collective, Development Seed, TechSoup Europe, Code for Poland – we started this project together and now we’d love you to give us a hand!

 

Because of the recent (February 2014) massive protests and the almost not at all addressed damages caused by the last spring floods, pool results show that most Bosnians haven’t decided which parties to support in the upcoming elections, as they usually would have by now. Given that Zasto ne, a leader of the project, also is a local leader in using new technologies for deepening democracy – this technologically enabled attempt to establish a transparent communication with the political parties and open up the Bosnian government, has big chances of becoming successful. Let’s spur innovation and make change, which is what most of us hope for.

 

Why are we doing it?

 

Bosnia and Herzegovina, a country which resides “in the heart of the Balkans” is still struggling to recover from the ’92-’95 war and build a solid democratic state. BiH has one of the most complicated election’s system in the world. The country is composed by 2 entities and hosts 3 major ethnicities. It also elects 5 presidents and is managed by 14 governments…

Guided by a strong belief that access to public information (all information originating from the field of work of the public sector bodies) is a necessary condition for civic engagement, we are working on creating an open data platform which will host data from Bosnian elections since 2004 (+ the upcoming October 2014 general elections data). The purpose of the platform is to open data up.  We want to make it available (cleaned, refined and ready for pulling out through an API) for organizations and individuals (journalists, researchers, hactivists) interested and advanced in the topic. We also want data to become understandable for the wider audience (for which purpose we need mash-ups, infographics and nice data visualizations).

 

How can you help?

 

Depends on how you’d like to help Any support or advice which you can offer will be highly appreciated! During the 4 days which we spent working on the platform we defined main goals, audiences and an MVP data storytelling plan. We also shot this short video which will serve as a storyboard material for an actual informatory clip about the elections.

 

 

 

For more technical details please check out our GitHub repository. If you have any additional questions, please tweet to anyone from the group (handles are to be found in the first paragraph) or send an e-mail to anna.kuliberda@gmail.com.
