About Code for Freedom

Code for Freedom is an international Hackathon organised to promote the ideals of freedom, solidarity and democracy in the 21st century. The first edition of Code for Freedom will take place in Gdańsk (Poland), on September 27-28, 2014 at the European Solidarity Centre. The event is organised by the Lech Walesa Institute and the ePaństwo Foundation in cooperation with Teplitsa (Greenhouse) of Social Technologies.

 

We chose Gdańsk as the location for this conceptual Hackathon, as it is the cradle of one of the most important socio-political movements in the last century: Solidarność (Solidarity). Solidarność was born over 30 years ago, when workers at the Gdańsk Shipyard made a collective effort to stand up against the unsovereign government of the Polish People’s Republic to fight for their rights, effectively starting a domino effect which brought about the end of communism in Eastern and Central Europe by 1989.

 

The actions of the pioneers of Solidarność have inspired us to organise Code for Freedom. We believe that this movement’s ideals,  combined with the technological advancements of the 21st century, have the power to change the world. Bringing together the talents of activists, coders and graphic designers from across the globe — to a place remembered for a historic and peaceful socio-political transformation — has the potential to generate a great number of technological tools and applications, which can help people all over the world in their fight for freedom, democracy and solidarity.

 

Technology and activism go hand in hand, and together can ameliorate many aspects of our everyday struggles for a better world. Empowering human rights, responding to humanitarian disasters, reducing life loss in underprivileged regions of the world and many more can be the addressed with these applications.

 

Since ideas are fundamental to our cause, we encourage you to participate in this Hackathon and bring your own idea to the table. Anyone who comes to our event with an idea of how to use technology to improve world as we know it will have a chance to work with coders, programmers and activists, and possibly create an application which will fulfil that goal.

 

We are certain that the 2 days that activists and hackers will have to create their apps will allow for the creation of many technological solutions, which will help people all over the world to create a better and more peaceful world.

 

We expect approximately 100 participants, coders, graphic designers and activists, all of whom will be involved in the creation of these applications.

 

Join us in Gdańsk on September 27-28 and help us create applications that will advance the ideals of  freedom, solidarity and democracy and change the world for better.
